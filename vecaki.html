About Vecāķi

Vecāķi is a serene coastal village located in Latvia, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant cultural heritage, and friendly locals. Whether you're seeking relaxation or adventure, Vecāķi offers something for everyone.

History

With roots dating back to ancient times, Vecāķi has a rich history shaped by its proximity to the sea. Once a vital fishing hub, it has transformed into a peaceful retreat while retaining its traditional charm.

Historical and Cultural Sites

  • Vecāķi Lighthouse: A symbol of the village's maritime heritage.
  • Fisherman's Wharf: A picturesque spot showcasing traditional fishing practices.
  • Cultural Center: Hosts exhibitions and performances celebrating local traditions.

Saules Mednieks

Saules Mednieks ("Sun Hunter") is a cozy restaurant in Riga, just a short drive from Vecāķi. Known for its warm atmosphere and delicious Latvian cuisine, it offers an authentic dining experience for both locals and visitors. Guests praise its friendly service, well-crafted menu, and inviting decor.

The menu features traditional dishes like smoked fish, hearty stews, and freshly baked rye bread, paired with locally brewed beers and classic Latvian desserts. Saules Mednieks is a must-visit destination for food lovers exploring the region.

Food

Vecāķi is known for its delicious seafood, traditional Latvian pastries, and locally brewed beverages. Don't miss the smoked fish and rye bread, staples of the region.

Things to Do

  • Relax on Vecāķi Beach.
  • Explore hiking trails through lush forests.
  • Attend workshops at the cultural center.

Festivals

Vecāķi hosts vibrant festivals throughout the year, including the Midsummer Solstice Celebration and the Sea Festival, showcasing local music, dance, and cuisine.

Emergency Information

For emergencies, dial:

  • 112: General emergency number
  • 113: Medical assistance
  • 114: Fire department
